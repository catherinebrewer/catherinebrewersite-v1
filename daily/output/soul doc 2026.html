Values or traits I think I possess, in no particular order:

  • Curiosity. I like understanding the world and learning more.
  • Conviction, righteousness. I'm moved by suffering; it feels important and urgent to fix it. (With the corresponding downside of being preachy, stubborn, etc.)
    • Relative to others, I have a harder time doing "harm reduction", E.g., if for some reason it's important to act normal and happy when something is wrong in a situation. This can be true even if I intellectually believe that it would be better for me to act in this happy way.
  • Stubbornness. I am difficult to persuade out of values-judgements. But then also, I am somewhat persistent on them.
  • Fear of failure/easy embarrassment/low persistence on new-to-me skills/giving up easily when I'm not good at something.
  • Strength of feeling. I'm not often outwardly emotional, and I'm generally somewhat reserved in terms of who I discuss my feelings with. But I'm easily emotionally moved. My feelings can change frequently, and I feel them intensely.
  • Ambitious. This feels like a pretty important part of me. I want to be the best, I want to have disgustingly high ambition, I'm not satisfied with things less than that, and I'm thinking about how to get better all the time.
    • I think it's currently in service of this whole conviction thing and feels like I'm fighting for my values, but I can imagine this changing. From my current perspective, I feel better about this when it's behind my values and feel a bit more neutral about value-neutral ambition?
    • Also, this is not quite the same thing as, say, wanting to make lots of money. When I was a child, for example, I was ambitious in the direction of wanting to be a big rock star and not wanting to have anyone tell me what to do.
    • Also, I think I've gotten more ambitious over time and endorse it!
  • Analytical. I am pretty awkward about my feelings, and I think struggle to be "in my body." This is a purely negative framing. The positive framing is: I like talking and thinking about big complicated ideas, I generally think I'm pretty smart and good at abstract reasoning, I make sense of myself and my life in this way, etc.
  • Quick on the uptake. Especially relative to something like my overall intelligence, I think I'm pretty quick to pick things up.
  • (Relatively) impatient.
  • Loyalty.
    • don't really know how to describe this one because whenever I try to describe it, it feels a bit like I'm just describing a thing that everyone has. There's some reason to be suspicious that I actually have this trait! But I guess it feels to me that although I don't make tonnes of friendships, they often matter a lot to me. These people tend to stay in my life for a long time, and I don't tend to have drama with them. I want to back them. I want to be helpful to them. My guess is this is more of a felt preference within me rather than something I act on.
  • Somewhat lazy.
    • If a thing can be done poorly, or done last minute, or not done at all, I'm in favour of that!
  • Somewhat melancholy, maudlin, prone to depressive states.
  • Introspective, and pretty good at it. (In the sense that: I accurately identify what's going on myself, I'm pretty well calibrated, I'm able to perceive strengths and weaknesses that I think other people might miss.)
  • Moved by art. ~Spiritual, if you like.
  • (Increasing over time, I blame my work) Direct, mildly contrarian, values intellectual honesty a lot. I like examples and clarity. I don't mind if people are blunt.
  • Prone to overanalysis, daydreaming, overengineering. Better at thinking about things than executing.
  • High standards. Lots of descriptions of perfectionists feel like caricatures to me, and I think especially relative to most perfectionists, I'm less detail-oriented and care less about the final stages of shipping stuff, but I have high standards and want to do excellent work.
  • Morally serious.
  • Loving. Many of the most meaningful and important parts of my life are my relationships with my close family, my significant other, and my closest friends.

Values or traits which I want to do differently next year:

  • Maintain: ambition, moral seriousness.
  • Cultivate: loyalty/being a good and reliable friend, resilience/persistence, patience.
  • Let go of: high standards (c.f. photography class lesson), reservedness (unless it's load-bearing).
