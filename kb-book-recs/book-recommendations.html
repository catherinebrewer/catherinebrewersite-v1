Catherine's Recommendations for Mum, 2025

Happy Christmas! I've done some research and reading, and here are some books I think you'll enjoy. Click on the covers to see more details about them and a link to get them.

Everything Is Tuberculosis

by John Green

John Green's story of Henry, a boy he meets, and the history of humanity overcoming tuberculosis. One of my favourite science books this year.

The Britannias

by Alice Albinia

A history and travelogue of Britain's islands. I thought you'd enjoy this especially given Jersey!

Material World

by Ed Conway

A history of the world and of global politics via six substances: sand, salt, iron, copper, oil, and lithium. I haven't gotten a chance to read this myself, but I've heard it highly recommended for fans of Prisoners of Geography and of Breakneck.

Crashed

by Adam Tooze

Adam Tooze is kind of insane but in a fun way; if you want something to chew on, this big history of the 2008 financial crisis will do the trick!

Say Nothing

by Patrick Radden Keefe

By the same author as Empire of Pain, a history of the Troubles. Probably not the cheeriest read, but I remembered how much you enjoyed Empire of Pain and thought you would also like this.

Annals of the Former World

John McPhee

John McPhee won the Pulitzer Prize for this, a story of the geological formation of North America across 4.6 billion years. It has some of the loveliest prose I've found in non-fiction, and (despite what you might expect) is fascinating throughout.
